# Law on Personal Data Protection (Vietnam Law 91/2025/QH15)

## 1. Conceptual Section (Teach Mode)

### 1.1 Overview & Scope

> [!Definition] ðŸ“œ Legal Status
> **Law No:** 91/2025/QH15
> **Promulgated:** June 26, 2025
> **Effective Date:** January 1, 2026 
> **Scope:** Applies to Vietnamese and foreign agencies/individuals in Vietnam, and foreign entities processing data of Vietnamese citizens living in Vietnam .

### 1.2 Key Definitions (Article 2)

* **Personal Data:** Information that identifies or helps identify a specific person.
    * **Basic Personal Data:** Common info used in transactions.
    * **Sensitive Personal Data:** Data associated with privacy which, if infringed, directly affects legitimate rights (e.g., health, finance, location).
* **Processing:** Any activity affecting data: collection, analysis, encryption, storage, deletion, transfer, etc..
* **Controller:** Decides purposes and means.
* **Processor:** Processes data on behalf of the controller.
* **De-identification:** Changing data so it cannot identify a person without additional info.

---

### 1.3 Rights and Obligations (Article 4)

> [!Concept] âš–ï¸ Rights of Data Subjects
> 1.  **Right to Know:** Be informed about processing.
> 2.  **Consent Control:** Agree, disagree, or **withdraw consent**.
> 3.  **Access & Edit:** View, edit, or request correction.
> 4.  **Deletion:** Request provision, deletion, or restriction of processing.
> 5.  **Complaint:** Lodge complaints or lawsuits for damages.

* **Obligations:** Subjects must provide accurate data and respect others' data .

---

### 1.4 Processing Conditions

#### Consent-Based Processing (Article 9)
* **Voluntary & Informed:** Consent is only valid if voluntary and the subject knows the type of data, purpose, and rights .
* **Explicit:** Must be clear, specific, and printable/copyable. **Silence is NOT consent**.
* **Withdrawal:** Subjects can request withdrawal of consent. Processing must stop (unless exceptions apply), but prior processing remains valid.

#### Processing WITHOUT Consent (Article 19)
Consent is **not required** in specific cases :
1.  **Emergency:** Protecting life, health, or vital interests in urgent cases.
2.  **National Security:** Preventing crime, terrorism, or riots.
3.  **State Management:** Serving state agency activities prescribed by law.
4.  **Contract:** Fulfilling contractual agreements with the subject.

---

### 1.5 Cross-Border Transfers (Article 20)

> [!Warning] ðŸŒ Transfer Requirements
> Transferring data outside Vietnam requires strict compliance:
> 1.  **Impact Assessment (Dossier):** Must compile a dossier assessing risks.
> 2.  **Submission:** Send the original dossier to the specialized agency (Ministry of Public Security) within **60 days** of the transfer.
> 3.  **Updates:** Update the dossier every 6 months if there are changes.

**Exceptions to Assessment:** State agencies, internal employee data on cloud services, or personal transfers by the subject .

---

### 1.6 Specific Sector Regulations

| Sector | Key Regulation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Marketing / Ads** | Must have **Consent**; users must be able to **Opt-out**; no "sub-leasing" data without permission . |
| **Finance / Banking** | No credit scoring without consent; mandatory notification of account data loss . |
| **HR / Employment** | Delete data if not hired (unless agreed); delete data upon contract termination. |
| **Children (Art 24)** | Processing requires consent of the child (if $\ge$ 7 years old) **AND** the legal representative. |
| **AI / Big Data** | Must not use AI/Big Data to infringe on national security or honor/dignity. AI processing must be classified by **risk level** . |

---

### 1.7 Penalties (Article 8)

> [!Alert] ðŸ’¸ Fines & Sanctions
> * **Max Fine (General):** Up to **3 Billion VND**.
> * **Buying/Selling Data:** Fine up to **10 times** the revenue from the violation.
> * **Cross-Border Violations:** Fine up to **5% of the preceding year's turnover**.

## 2. ðŸ“˜ Examples & Applications (Exam Mode)

### Example 1: Processing Without Consent
> **Using:** Article 19 (Exceptions to Consent) 
>
> **Scenario:** A hospital receives an unconscious patient from a car accident. They process his ID and medical history to perform emergency surgery without his consent.
>
> **Analysis:**
> * **General Rule:** Processing usually requires consent (Art 9).
> * **Exception:** Article 19(1)(a) allows processing to "protect the life, health... in urgent cases."
> * **Conclusion:** The hospital's action is **lawful**.

### Example 2: Cross-Border Transfer Compliance
> **Using:** Article 20 (Cross-Border Transfer) 
>
> **Scenario:** A Vietnamese e-commerce startup stores customer data on AWS servers in Singapore.
>
> **Requirement:**
> 1.  This is a "transfer of personal data stored in Vietnam to a data storage system located outside" (Art 20(1)(a)).
> 2.  **Action:** They must submit an **Impact Assessment Dossier** to the Ministry of Public Security within **60 days** of starting the transfer.
> 3.  **Maintenance:** They must update this dossier every **6 months** if there are changes.

### Example 3: Marketing & Opt-Out
> **Using:** Article 28 (Advertising) 
>
> **Scenario:** A bank sends credit card offers to a customer via SMS. The customer replies "STOP". The bank continues sending messages, claiming they have a "legitimate interest."
>
> **Analysis:**
> * **Violation:** Article 28(5) states organizations must "stop advertising at the request of personal data subjects."
> * **Requirement:** Advertising requires consent and a mechanism to opt-out.
> * **Conclusion:** The bank is violating the law.

---

## 3. Summary

* **Core Rights:** Subjects have full control (Access, Edit, Delete, Withdraw Consent).
* **Consent:** Must be explicit and active (silence $\ne$ consent).
* **Exceptions:** Emergencies, National Security, and Contract Performance do not require separate consent.
* **International:** Transferring data abroad is permitted but heavily regulated (Impact Assessment Dossier required).
* **Notification:** Violations must be reported to the authority within **72 hours**.
* **Liability:** High financial penalties for trading data (10x revenue) or violating cross-border rules (5% turnover).****
